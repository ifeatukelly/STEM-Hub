Resources for Students
Lets take a look at the four different types of learning styles to see how you prefer to learn: Visual, Auditory, Reading and Writing, or Kinesthetic.
Visual Learner
You remember information the best when you can see it - for example, clear pictures, animations or diagrams.
Auditory Learner
You remember information the best when you can hear it - for example, listening to other people talking or watching videos.
Reading and Writing Learner
You remember information the best when you read it or write it out - for example, worksheets or taking notes.
Kinesthetic Learner
You remember information best when you are hands-on and physically do it yourself - for example, activities or lab work.

Resources for Parents
How can you get involved at home to support your child get excited with STEM?
Urge Curiosity
Embrace the constant "why's" and "how's" and allow them to explore their ideas safely.
Encourage Engineer Thinking
Let them go through their own decision making process by guiding them to think of the pros and cons before coming to a solution.
Engage In Activities
Go through the activities in the 'Resources for Students' section with them to stimulate their minds and curiosity.
The Institute of Imagination has many different resources for you to further stimulate your child's mind with fun activities they can enjoy all year round
Indy's Child has a variety of fun acitivites you can do with your child, as well as different ways to implement STEM in your child's mind in primary education at home.
